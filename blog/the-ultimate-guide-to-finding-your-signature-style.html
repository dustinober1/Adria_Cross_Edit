The Ultimate Guide to Finding Your Signature Style

Published on December 24, 2025 • By Adria Cross (Updated December 24, 2025)

Do you ever open your closet, see racks full of clothes, and still feel like you have absolutely nothing to wear? We’ve all been there. It’s usually not a lack of options, but a lack of direction.


Finding your signature style isn't about following every trend that pops up on social media (looking at you, [Current Year] trends!). It’s about curating a wardrobe that reflects who you are, fits your lifestyle, and makes you feel unstoppable.

Here is a simple roadmap to start discovering a style that is uniquely yours.


1. Analyze Your "Repeat Offenders"

Before you go shopping, look at what you already own. Pull out the 5-10 items you reach for constantly. Is it a structured blazer? A pair of vintage jeans? A floral midi dress?

  • Ask yourself: Why do I love these? Is it the fabric? The fit? The color?
  • The Takeaway: These items are the breadcrumbs leading to your true style preferences.


2. Define Your "Three Words"

This is a favorite stylist trick. If you had to describe your ideal look in just three words, what would they be?

  • Examples: "Classic, Neutral, Comfortable" or "Bold, Artistic, Edgy."
  • Try it: Next time you want to buy something, ask: "Does this piece fit my three words?" If not, put it back.


3. Prioritize Your Lifestyle

Your fantasy self might wear silk gowns to gala dinners, but does your real self spend 8 hours a day in an office or chasing toddlers? A signature style must be functional. If you love the look of high heels but walk 5 miles a day, your signature style might actually be "Chic Sneakers" or "Polished Loafers." Embrace what actually works for your day-to-day life.


4. Create a Mood Board

You don't need a physical corkboard. A simple Pinterest board or an "Inspiration" folder on Instagram works wonders. Save looks that catch your eye instantly. After a few weeks, look for patterns. Are you pinning mostly monochromatic outfits? Lots of layers? Bright accessories? This visual data is pure gold.


Ready to Edit Your Wardrobe?

Defining your style is a journey, not a sprint. If you feel stuck or overwhelmed by the choices, sometimes you need a fresh set of eyes.
